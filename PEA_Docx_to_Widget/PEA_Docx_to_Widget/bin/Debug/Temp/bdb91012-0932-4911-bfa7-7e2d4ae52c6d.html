Main-text

The world around us is made up of energy and matter. Matter is the ‘stuff’ around us.

Some of this stuff we can’t see, like gases, but we can feel these gases as we move through them, or they move through us. Some solid stuff is too heavy to pick up, some is so hard it can cut us, but other solids can feel soft against our skin. Some liquid stuff flows through pipes, rivers and our bodies, their journeys powered by forces such as gravity.

All this stuff, or matter, is made up of particles. It is these particles that we feel when the wind blows, and it is these particles that can hurt or comfort us.

In this topic you will learn about particles and how to explain and predict the <gloss:properties|properties> of matter, the stuff around us.
